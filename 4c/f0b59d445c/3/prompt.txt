## Revision 5: Subtle paper texture

Add a faint paper-like texture to both artboard backgrounds so they feel like manuscript paper rather than flat digital surfaces.

**Approach:** An overlay div covering the full artboard with a very subtle CSS noise/grain pattern at extremely low opacity. This sits behind all content but on top of the flat background color.

**Light (F):** A full-size absolute-positioned div with a subtle repeating radial-gradient pattern (tiny dots in slightly darker warm tone `#F0EFE9` at ~30% opacity) creating a fine paper grain. Barely perceptible — you feel it more than see it.

**Dark (G):** Same technique but inverted — tiny lighter specks (`#2A2621` dots) against the walnut. Even more subtle since dark surfaces need less texture to read as material.

**Execution:** Add a single overlay div as the first child of each artboard, absolutely positioned, full width/height, with `pointer-events: none` conceptually (it won't block interaction in Paper since it's just a visual). Use CSS `background-image` with a fine repeating `radial-gradient` pattern.
