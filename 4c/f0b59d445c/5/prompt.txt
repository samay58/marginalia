## Direction D: "The Study" — Evening reading by lamplight

A dark-mode direction, but NOT code-editor dark. The feeling of reading proofs late at night at a warm desk. Walnut and aged leather, not charcoal and neon.

**Palette:**
- Background: `#2C2620` (deep warm walnut)
- Text: `#E8E0D4` (warm cream on dark)
- Redline: `#D4775A` (warm coral — luminous against the dark, like a red pencil catching lamplight)
- Additions: `#C9B896` (aged gold/parchment — subtle warmth, like pencil-in-margin)
- Chrome: `#6B6158` (muted warm brown)
- Dividers: `#3D352E` (barely visible warm rule)

**Typography:**
- Body: Crimson Pro, 400, 18px, line-height 30px — elegant, high-contrast serif that reads beautifully on dark backgrounds
- Headings: Crimson Pro, 700, 32px — dramatic weight contrast
- UI: DM Sans, 300, 11px — whisper-quiet
- Annotations: Crimson Pro, 400 italic, 14px — warm, personal

**Layout:** 1040x780. Same three-zone structure but the margins feel darker, more enveloping. The text column is a pool of warm light in a dark room. The annotation in the margin has a subtle warm glow (very faint background tint).

**What makes it different:** Every other direction is light-mode. This one explores whether the editorial intimacy can survive (and even deepen) in a dark environment. The redline marks should feel luminous — not error-red but ember-warm, like a mark that catches the light. The emotional register shifts from "desk in daylight" to "desk at midnight."

**Content:** Same memo passage (inference infrastructure) with the same two edits and margin note.

## Direction E: "The Proof Sheet" — High-contrast modernist editorial

The opposite of cozy. Stark, precise, authoritative. A freshly printed proof sheet under bright light. Think Penguin Modern Classics cover design, or a broadsheet editorial page. Monochrome except for a single, confident red.

**Palette:**
- Background: `#FCFCFA` (near-white, bright but not clinical)
- Text: `#141414` (near-black, very high contrast)
- Redline: `#CC3322` (single saturated editorial red — the ONLY color)
- Additions: `#141414` (same as body text, medium weight — clean text = approved)
- Chrome: `#B0B0B0` (neutral mid-gray)
- Dividers: `#141414` (strong black rules — structural, not decorative)

**Typography:**
- Body: Charter, 400, 17px, line-height 28px — designed for clarity at small sizes, workhorse editorial serif, Matthew Carter precision
- Headings: Charter, 900 (Black), 28px, letter-spacing -0.02em — heavy and authoritative
- UI: IBM Plex Sans, 400, 10px, letter-spacing 0.06em, uppercase — production metadata
- Annotations: Charter, 400 italic, 13px — brief, sharp

**Layout:** 1040x780. Distinctive feature: a strong horizontal rule under the title (2px black), and another above the status bar. The manuscript column is tighter (~500px). Wide empty margins that feel intentional and modernist, not just unused space. The annotation sits in the right margin with a thin vertical red rule as its only decoration.

**What makes it different:** Maximum contrast, minimum decoration. One color (red) against monochrome. Strong structural rules. Charter's workmanlike clarity vs. the more decorative serifs in other directions. The mood is not warm or intimate — it's precise, awake, almost severe. The pleasure here isn't coziness, it's the satisfaction of a perfectly sharp pencil on bright white paper.

**Content:** Same memo passage, same edits, same margin note.
